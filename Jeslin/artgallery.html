Here are the main 8 Phases of our natural satellite Moon
New Moon

The New Moon is the first lunar phase when the Moon is not visible from Earth. It marks the beginning of a new lunar cycle.

Waxing Crescent

The Waxing Crescent is the second lunar phase when a small, illuminated portion of the Moon becomes visible after the New Moon.

First Quarter

The First Quarter Moon is when half of the Moon is visible from Earth. It is also known as the Half Moon.

Waxing Gibbous

The Waxing Gibbous is the third lunar phase when more than half but not all of the Moon's illuminated portion is visible from Earth.

Full Moon

The Full Moon is the lunar phase when the Moon appears fully illuminated from Earth's perspective. It occurs approximately every 29.5 days.

Waning Gibbous

The Waning Gibbous is the fourth lunar phase when more than half but not all of the Moon's illuminated portion is visible as it decreases in size.

Third Quarter

The Third Quarter Moon is when half of the Moon is visible from Earth, but in the opposite direction compared to the First Quarter Moon.

Waning Crescent

The Waning Crescent is the fifth and final lunar phase when a small, illuminated portion of the Moon becomes visible before the New Moon.

New Moon

Then again the new moon phase comes and it repeats the cycle for everymonth until the cycle ends.
